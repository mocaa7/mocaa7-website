User Experience / Product Designer

Seattle, Washington

I approach new designs like a child approaches the world: asking WHY. Their thoughts are free from assumptions that we often take for granted. However, we are inevitably biased. A clear perspective means evaluating the presence of our biases and how they are affecting our designs, and ultimately, our thoughts.

UX Objective

Finding the user: identifying the RIGHT audience and immersing myself in their mindset
Tailor solutions to users: a child-like open-mindedness and directed empathy
Accounts for the entirety of the experience: context, emotions, goals and motivations

Work approach

Driven to find the pattern: can this method apply across various contexts?
Focuses on impact: how would this task contribute to the final product/service?
Applies logic: is this argument valid? Is it sound?
Mindful of effects: what effects am I making beyond this single context?

UX is everywhere. With my experience as a distress services volunteer and peer supporter, I was dealing with a diversity of people. Every person has their own model of the world, and consequently, their own biases. Support meant sensitivity to the unknown: their subjective experiences. I had to capture my biases and acknowledge their presence. That is the key to open-mindedness.
