BFG is a weight neutral fitness app for plus-size women. Contrary to other fitness apps, "success" is measured with metrics that exclude weight. It creates an encouraging environment for users to thrive in their fitness lives.

To design an effective app, we need to understand the ideal environment for these women to thrive in their fitness lives.

In this project, the BFG app was designed to:
(1) Encourage plus-size women to exercise
(2) Increase self-confidence through a positive mindset
(3) Build a support network

Read full case study on medium.

Design Goal

The design should encourage plus-size women to exercise, increase self-confidence through a positive mindset, and build a support network.

The Clients

Louis Green, the founder (also a fitness trainer specialized in plus-size women)
Tarryn Rudolph, recently teamed up with Louise (also a nurse)

Due to their past struggles with fitness, they are very devoted to their work.

Anyone can be an athlete, no matter their shape or size.

They want to:

(1) Increase brand recognition
(2) Showcase what their brand is about

The User

Primary audience: plus-size women already part of their Facebook BFG group.
We interviewed 7 users.

Key Findings

Best motivated by: a supportive community
Exercise goals: personal/internal fulfillment
Ideal fitness environment: authentic, individually tailored, health focused
Motivators: approach oriented, positivity
Mindset: self-acceptance, positivity

The Problem

Want to keep fit, healthy and confident through support.

Being there for you, not being there for calorie burn and it doesn't matter who you are but... everybody works at their own pace, and everybody does what they want to do. So just trying to encourage people in the most positive way and get them to work wherever their level is.

Scenarios

  1. Mika is part of the BFG Facebook page. She loves its vibes and can relate with its members. She wants to form a workout group with BFG members in her city. However, there are too many members to look through.
  2. Sarah wants to start walking home on the daily. However, she often gets discouraged and wants an accountability group within the BFG community to keep her on track. She feels safe to share her struggles with them.

Our Solution

Create an app that can encourage users to exercise:

(1) Through community challenges, both online and in-person
(2) By building a postive mindset

Online community challenges: Sarah can engage in her own exercise activity while enjoying the support of others through online accountability.
Meet-up community challenges: Mika can exercise with locals in her city to whom she can relate; people with a mindset promoted by the BFG brand.
Building a positive mindset: this app gets you in a positive mindset from the moment you open it. Language and imagery create positive vibes for the users.

Information Architecture

The information architecture highlights the three solutions provided by the app: setting and keeping a positive mindset, a sense of community (through group challenges), and accountability (by setting goals).

Creating an account (user activation)

  • Set the positive mindset: process of creating a profile, agreeing to community standards

Using the app (user engagement & retention)

  • Community: creating challenges
  • Keep the positive mindset: positive mantras
  • Accountability: setting goals and completing them
